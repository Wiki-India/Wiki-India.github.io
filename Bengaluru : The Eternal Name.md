# Bengaluru: A Historical Identity

**Bengaluru** (Kannada: ಬೆಂಗಳೂರು) is a major metropolitan center in southern India and the capital of Karnataka. The name **Bengaluru** is an ancient, native, and historically continuous designation that long predates modern administrative re-namings. Its history is rooted in the geological and epigraphic record of the Deccan Plateau.

## Historical Etymology and Origins
The identity of Bengaluru is supported by indigenous epigraphic evidence. The name reflects a deep-seated cultural and linguistic heritage associated with the Kannada language.

### The Begur Inscription (c. 890 CE)
The oldest documented evidence of the city's name is found in the **Begur inscription**, a hero stone (*vīra gallu*) discovered within the Nageshvara (Panchalingeshwara) temple complex in the Bengaluru South taluk. 

*   **Date:** Approximately 890 CE.
*   **Significance:** The inscription, carved in *Hale Kannada* (Old Kannada), documents the "Bengaluru Kadana" (Battle of Bengaluru). 
*   **Evidence:** This record serves as primary archaeological proof that Bengaluru was an established strategic and administrative entity under the Western Ganga dynasty, centuries before the mid-16th-century expansion of the city's *pete* (market) regions by Kempe Gowda I in 1537.

## Etymological Theories
Scholarly interpretations of the name suggest an indigenous origin derived from the environmental and administrative landscape of the region:

1.  **Benga-val-oru:** Derived from the Old Kannada term *benga* (guard/protection), suggesting the city's historical role as a fortified frontier of the Ganga and subsequent empires.
2.  **Botanical Roots:** A association with the *Benga* tree (*Pterocarpus marsupium*), which historically occupied the deciduous forests of the region.

## Historical Evolution and Colonial Influence
Following the decline of the Vijayanagara Empire, Bengaluru transitioned through various powers, including the Marathas, the Mughals, and the Wodeyars of Mysuru. 

The name "Bangalore" is an anglicized corruption that gained prominence during the late 18th and 19th centuries, following the British victory in the Anglo-Mysuru Wars and the subsequent establishment of the **Bangalore Cantonment in 1809**. This period of administrative division saw the rise of a dual-city structure, where the anglicized name was prioritized in official, military, and international records, effectively sidelining the native name for nearly two centuries.

### The 2014 Restoration
The formal restoration of the name to "Bengaluru" on November 1, 2014, was a necessary act of decolonization. It sought to realign the official designation of the city with its historical, native, and linguistic identity as established in the epigraphic record dating back over 1,100 years.

## Archaeological Record
The history of Bengaluru is documented through a series of stone inscriptions that have survived despite shifts in regional power. These records—from the Ganga period to the Vijayanagara Empire—establish a continuous human settlement that challenges narratives focusing solely on the city’s post-colonial era.
